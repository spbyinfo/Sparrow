## 🔑 Key Principles for "Perishable" Services

1. **Time-Sensitive Value**
   - Must lose value if time passes without booking
   - Last-minute availability creates urgency

2. **Capacity-Based**
   - Service has fixed slots/seats/space
   - Empty capacity = wasted opportunity cost

3. **Win-Win Model**
   - Business fills empty slots (revenue > $0)
   - Users get discounts (value for green credits)
   - Sparrow facilitates sustainable consumption

4. **Location-Based**
   - Services must be local and accessible
   - Distance matters for user convenience

5. **Quality Maintained**
   - Discount doesn't mean lower quality
   - Same service, just unsold inventory

## 📊 Credit Pricing Strategy

**Low Credits (200-500):**
- Short duration services
- High frequency (daily availability)
- Examples: Salon visit, car wash, cafe session

**Medium Credits (500-1000):**
- Medium duration or specialty
- Weekly/few times per week
- Examples: Yoga class, cooking workshop, photography

**High Credits (1000-2000):**
- Premium services
- Rare availability or high value
- Examples: Concert tickets, spa package, consultation

---

## 🎯 Implementation Notes

- All services show **"Available today"** when slots exist
- Display **"X left"** to create urgency
- Use **distance** to help users choose nearby
- Filter by **category** using horizontal scroll icons
- **Favorites** feature for repeat bookings
- **Rating** system for quality assurance

---

**Philosophy:** Help businesses monetize empty capacity while rewarding eco-conscious users with valuable experiences! 🌱✨
